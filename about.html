About Dr. Alexia M. Hernández

Dr. Alexia M. Hernández is a linguistic consultant specializing in AI applications, with expertise in speech variation, accents, and human–machine communication. Her work focuses on helping organizations develop AI systems that understand the diversity of human voice and text.

Alexia holds an MBA from the Wharton School and a PhD in Linguistics from Stanford University, where she researched how people produce and perceive speech variation and accents—work that integrates sociolinguistics, cognitive science, and experimental methods. She graduated summa cum laude and Phi Beta Kappa from Princeton University with a BA in Linguistics, earning top departmental prizes for her research.

Her research has been presented at leading conferences such as the Linguistic Society of America, Psychonomics, and New Ways of Analyzing Variation, and published in respected venues including the Journal of Cognitive Science. Her work advances understanding of how listeners make rapid inferences based on voice alone, and how these perceptual processes shape real-world communication and technology design.

At LexAI Consulting, Alexia brings this expertise into applied settings—collaborating with product, research, and strategy teams to evaluate AI-generated language, analyze speech data, improve voice interfaces, and design evaluation frameworks that reflect real human communication. Her work bridges rigorous linguistic research with practical, user-centered recommendations.

With experience across academia, tech, AI strategy, and language-focused research roles, Alexia offers a rare combination of analytic rigor, linguistic depth, and product-aligned thinking. She works with organizations seeking clarity in complex language questions, better human–AI interaction, and linguistically informed system design.
